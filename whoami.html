My core values

Adventure, Humility, Altruism, self-discipline and trust/reliability

Once I had gone on my first travel adventure, little has motivated me in my adult life as much as travelling more has. And it isnt the act of scanning a ticket and sitting on some plane seat for hours on end or spending even more time in airports crawling through processing and luggage recovery. Nor is it Hotels, room service, theme parks, Bars or shops. buildings or landmarks, none of that.

Its the type of humanity that gets presented to you arrive. When it is obvious that you are a stranger and or new to that environment. The values I wrote up the top there almost always appear in the interactions with strangers when I am in a new place. And because of that I believe that they represent the best of humanity. A local host wants you, the stranger to trust them, and they in turn want to be able to trust you.  They will altruistically go out of their way to take you to shelter or somewhere to eat or even just help you get to somewhere where you can acquire those services more easily. They will show their humility when showing you around their environment, and when ask you about the environment you come from, equally want to see that humility from you along with some interesting information in your description of your own environment. ( who wants to hear well yeah, where I come from its much better than hereits safer and cleaner and warmer and has Mcdonalds on every corner etc etc )

And when interacting with this stranger that is playing temporary host to you, they are happily and eagerly going on this small adventure with you as they interact with you and break away from the tediousness of everyday life. This is how my experiences have shaped the way I try to behave as a human towards everyone whether they are known to me or not.

 

 

My ethical decision making

Fortunately, no major ethical dilemmas have truly crossed my path in adulthood to the degree that the outcomes were potentially destructive to life or liberty of any other. But there is one that was made by me as a teenager that obviously had strong effects on both me, and my direct family. And will certainly be informative to those that read or assess this content.

As so many millions have had to go through, mine and my brothers parents went through a divorce.  Though it appeared civil enough on the surface, psychologically, it was damaging to a large degree, to my father especially. In those situations, emotions run high and decisions made hastily. My father moved out of the family home and lifestyle block (small farm) that they had worked so much on; to the spare bedroom of his parents house. I went to see him there after school one day, in a state not worth describing any more than your own imagination could put together itself. It was not what anyone would want to see of their parental figure. Upon witnessing this and assessing the state of my mother back at the family home, I proceeded to instruct my mother to pack her things and leave the house so that my father could return. To some degree of surprise to me, she did as I told. I made that decision based on the belief that my mother was less likely to suffer as much as my father would have, from having to live elsewhere. By no means did this decision lead to rainbows, fairy tales and happy endings, and the consequences likely play out to this day for better or for worse, but I made it and as a much more experienced human today, I cant think of any reason to betray my younger self and change my decision.  I think of the two choices to speak up or do nothing, I did what my brain compelled me to do and that was for the best, in the aggregate.

An example of trying to work with others but running into resistance or tension

Opening of a new location of a restaurant bar in UK where a selection of our team including me from the original location, were sent to run without anyone being given the position of front of house manager or team leader.

Each of us had slightly different ideas on how running the till system and service should work, and each implemented their own style when they were working. Quickly I saw this as inefficient and was causing inconsistent service times. So, I started to take charge and set one system that we all were to adhere to. This wasnt easily accepted by the other male team member and though I stated clearly why this was preferable for better service to the customers and communication with the kitchen, he did not agree and returned back to the original location. I continued to run the location for the duration of my stay in London with a different team and continued on until the location was forced out by the university that controlled the building as it was the only business in the area not run by the University and therefore taking patronage and money away from its locations.
